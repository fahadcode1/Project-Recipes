Chicken Biryani

Home

Discription

Chicken Biryani is a world-famous South Asian rice dish made with fragrant basmati rice, tender chicken, and a blend of aromatic spices. Layered with fried onions, fresh herbs, and saffron-infused rice, it’s a festive meal that’s both rich in flavor and tradition. Perfect for family gatherings, celebrations, or a special weekend treat, chicken biryani is loved for its irresistible aroma and delicious taste.

ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500 g (about 1 lb) chicken, cut into medium pieces
  • 3 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3–4 cloves
  • 2–3 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A few fresh mint leaves
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water

    • Steps

  • Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  • Heat oil or ghee in a large pan. Fry the sliced onions until golden brown and set half aside for garnishing.
  • In the same pan, add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon. Sauté until fragrant.
  • Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for 1–2 minutes.
  • Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for 1–2 minutes.
  • Add chicken pieces and cook until they turn white on the outside.
  • Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for 3–4 minutes.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  • Stir in yogurt, mint, and coriander leaves. Cook until the chicken is tender and the oil separates.
  • In a separate pot, boil 4 cups of water with salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done. Drain.
  • Layer half of the parboiled rice over the chicken mixture. Sprinkle some of the fried onions, mint, coriander, garam masala, and saffron milk (if using).
  • Add the remaining rice on top, and repeat with onions, herbs, and saffron.
  • Cover the pot tightly and cook on low heat (“dum”) for 20–25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and infused with flavor./Li>
  • Gently fluff the rice and serve hot with raita or salad./Li>
